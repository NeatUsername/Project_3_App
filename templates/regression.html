Regression

Data Source: https://www.kaggle.com/fedesoriano/stroke-prediction-dataset


  • Clinical Dataset: Specific source and patient info unavailable.

  • Composed of 5,110 unique individual clinical records, with data regarding gender, age, hypertension, heart disease, marriage status, work type, residence, AVG glucose, smoking history, BMI, and stroke.

Machine Learning Process


Our team utilized the Python Packages Pandas and Scikit-learn to import, prepare, and model our dataset.


We started by exploring the data composition and constraints via Pandas; Knowing that our Categorical Data would need to be converted into a binary form, we cleaned the data series “gender” by purging a single “other” record, and then selected to reclassify smoking status from four conditions into two (i.e., “never smoked”, and “unknown” were grouped into “0” whereas “formerly smoked” and “smokes” were grouped into “1”. Ages were consolidated into various age groups for easier processing as well.


Our first attempt at building a predictive model was using Linear & Multilinear Regressions. We explored a Multilinear Model using five independent variables of gender, age groups, hypertension status, heart disease status, and the assigned smoking qualifier. Testing & Training Data appeared to track close visually, however our MSE and R2 Scores were 1.27 and 0.082, respectively. A secondary attempt using 3 independent variables of age group, hypertension, and the assigned smoking qualifier produced similar results (MSE and R2 Scores of 1.28 and 0.080). Then a linear model was constructed using the independent variable of age group, resulting in MSE and R2 of 1.29 and 0.075.


Toward the goal of securing a more accurate model we then transitioned toward a Logistic Model. In this attempt we selected independent variables of gender, hypertension, heart disease, and our assigned smoking qualifier. The model’s score for Training Data and Testing Data Accuracy was 0.95 and 0.94 respectively, significantly more accurate than prior attempts.
